Three scores. One number.

The Restaurant Confidence Score is a single 0–10 figure computed from three weighted components. The formula, the weights, and the version history are all public. No restaurant has ever paid for its ranking. None ever will.

Trust & Compliance · 40% Food safety & regulatory standing. FSA/FHRS hygiene ratings, structural compliance, confidence in management, inspection recency. Public-register data, decayed over time.
Customer Validation · 45% What real customers say. Bayesian-shrunk ratings from Google, TripAdvisor and OpenTable, with cross-source convergence flags when independent sources agree or disagree.
Commercial Readiness · 15% Is the venue set up to trade? Online presence, hours completeness, reservation & delivery paths, photo coverage, Companies House signals.
Modifier Capped editorial recognition. Michelin & AA distinctions contribute a small, bounded bonus. Awards can only move a venue inside its band — never buy a higher one. Read the full methodology.

Methodology · V3.4 → V4

Bayesian shrinkage replaces raw review averages.

Small-sample venues no longer outrank established ones on a handful of five-star reviews. The Customer Validation component now shrinks every rating toward the category prior.

Posted April 2026
Scoring · Temporal decay

Older inspections and reviews lose weight over time.

An exponential decay (λ = 0.0023 for FSA, 0.0046 for review recency) means a 300-day-old inspection contributes roughly half the signal of a fresh one — without being discarded.
