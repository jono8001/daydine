How does DayDine calculate the Restaurant Confidence Score?

A restaurant's standing in its town isn't the same as its follower count, or its busiest Saturday night. DayDine is built to reflect something quieter and more durable: the local market confidence around a venue — the composite view the public, the regulators, and the independent reviewers steadily form over time.

We reward restaurants that hold together under scrutiny. Clean inspections. Consistent reviews across different sources. A clear offer. The operational basics done well. Popularity, on its own, is not enough.

Why is your score different from TripAdvisor or Google ratings?

Five principles we apply to every score.

01
Multiple independent sources
No single source decides a position. A score is built from the agreement — or disagreement — of several unrelated data streams.
02
Freshness is weighted
A review or inspection from last month tells us more than one from two years ago. Older signals fade gracefully. They are never simply discarded.
03
Consistency across sources counts
When independent sources agree about a venue, confidence in the score rises. When they diverge, the score is adjusted to reflect that tension honestly.
04
Thin data is not overclaimed
A venue with little public information does not get to inherit a flattering score by default. We would rather say we don't know enough than flatter a ranking.
05
Ongoing calibration
The scoring model is reviewed and refined as new evidence arrives and real-world outcomes accumulate. A ranking that stops learning is a ranking that starts to lie.
Technical notes

DayDine applies time-based weighting so recent signals matter more than old ones; pairwise cross-source comparison so independent ratings check each other; confidence bands so thinly-evidenced venues are surfaced responsibly; and tiebreaker logic so no two venues share a position.

The specific constants, thresholds, and weightings behind these mechanisms are intentionally not published. See the note on proprietary logic below.

On confidence

Different venues, different amounts of evidence.

Some restaurants have years of reviews, clear inspection history, and a well-kept public profile. Others — a new café, a family pub without a website, an established room that has never chased coverage — have less of a paper trail.

DayDine reflects that honestly. Every ranked venue carries a confidence indicator alongside its position. Venues with limited public evidence are shown as such, not quietly downgraded. And where the evidence is simply too thin to rank responsibly, we say so rather than guess.

Fairness here means one thing: a restaurant's score should never pretend to know more than it does.

How often are restaurant scores updated?

Scores are recalculated weekly as new data arrives from public sources. FSA inspections, Google reviews, TripAdvisor ratings, and business filings are monitored continuously, and changes flow into the next scoring cycle.

Temporal decay is built in: a review or inspection from last month carries more weight than one from two years ago. Older signals fade gracefully — they are never simply discarded. This means a restaurant's score naturally responds to recent improvements or recent decline, without being whipsawed by a single data point.

DayDine is not the final word on food quality or the experience of sitting at a particular table on a particular night. It is a read on the public market confidence around a restaurant — the signals the wider world can see and the coherence of the picture those signals form together.

A note on integrity

Why we don't publish the full formula.

The broad principles behind the DayDine ranking are public, and they are explained above. The precise weightings, thresholds, and processing logic behind the score are not.

This is deliberate. A ranking that publishes its exact formula is a ranking that can be gamed — and a gamed ranking is useful to nobody: not diners, not the operators who play fair, and not the industry it exists to measure. Keeping the specifics private is how we keep the result honest.

We will always explain what the ranking considers. We will not explain it in a way that tells anyone how to manufacture an unearned position.
