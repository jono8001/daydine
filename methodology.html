Methodology

How We Rank

DayDine rankings are driven by the Restaurant Confidence Score (RCS) — a composite 0–10 metric that synthesises 40 public signals across 7 weighted tiers. The methodology is designed to reflect how a venue is actually performing in its local competitive landscape, not just how popular it is in isolation.

V3.4 · Last updated April 2026

The RCS is calculated to 3 decimal places on a 0–10 scale. Every ranked venue gets a unique position — a tiebreaker system guarantees no two restaurants share the same rank.

Temporal Decay

Signals are not all created equal across time. A 2023 inspection tells you less than a 2026 inspection. DayDine applies an exponential decay of the form e-λt to time-sensitive signals:

  • FSA inspection age: λ = 0.0023, a half-life of roughly 300 days. The final FSA tier score is an 80/20 blend of raw and decay-adjusted values.
  • Google review recency: λ = 0.0046, a half-life of roughly 150 days. Applied to the review volume signal when the latest review date is available.

Recent inspections and reviews carry full weight; two-year-old data loses 10–20% of its signal contribution. This prevents stale positive signals from propping up a venue that has drifted.

Cross-Source Convergence

When independent sources agree, confidence goes up. When they disagree, something needs explaining. DayDine compares normalised ratings pairwise across the FSA, Google, and TripAdvisor:

  • Converged (average divergence ≤ 0.10 on a 0–1 scale): +3% bonus to the final score.
  • Neutral (0.10–0.20): no adjustment.
  • Mild divergence (0.20–0.30): −3% penalty.
  • Strong divergence (> 0.30): −5% penalty.

A venue needs at least two independent sources for convergence scoring; single-source establishments receive no adjustment. This rewards consistency and flags data points that look off.

The Six Rating Bands

Final scores map to six rating bands on the 0–10 scale:

  • Excellent — 8.000 – 10.000
  • Good — 6.500 – 7.999
  • Generally Satisfactory — 5.000 – 6.499
  • Improvement Necessary — 3.500 – 4.999
  • Major Improvement — 2.000 – 3.499
  • Urgent Improvement — 0.000 – 1.999

Confidence Bands

Every ranked restaurant gets a confidence label based on how much data we were able to gather:

  • High confidence (±0.3): 20+ signals, 5+ active tiers.
  • Medium confidence (±0.5): 14+ signals, 4+ active tiers.
  • Low confidence (±0.8): at least 8 signals.
  • Insufficient data (not ranked): fewer than 8 signals — the venue is listed but excluded from rankings.

What We Don't Reveal

Our exact weighting model, signal processing algorithms, penalty rule constants, and competitive normalisation logic are proprietary. This preserves the integrity of the rankings and prevents the score from being gamed.
